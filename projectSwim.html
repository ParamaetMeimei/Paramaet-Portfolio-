Swimming Detection project

This project presents a novel AI-powered system for analyzing swimming videos to identify correct and incorrect swimming strokes. The system aims to assist swimming coaches in evaluating swimmers' techniques more effectively and accurately, while also providing tools for swimmers to improve their own strokes. The system employs a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) architecture trained on a large dataset of swimming videos. It is capable of recognizing four primary swimming strokes: freestyle, backstroke, butterfly, and breaststroke. Experimental results demonstrate the system's ability to accurately identify correct swimming strokes with high precision. The system offers valuable benefits for both swimming coaches and athletes by enhancing coaching practices, refining swimming skills, and reducing injury risks.
